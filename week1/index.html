Master Mobile UX

  • Mobile tracking has skyrocketed
  • "Whatever you are doing, do mobile first"
  • Just because a website is responsive, doesn't mean it is "mobile first"
      • Mobile first
    • time consuming
    • provides best experience
    • plans for the future and read for growth
  • desktop computer sales continually sink. Laptops have taken over computers
  • Start small and scale up. Concentrate on content and functionality

    • Understand users

    • Focus groups don't always work. People don't know what they want until they have it
    • Be there. Help them
    • Be useful. People should not need to hunt through the website. Provide what they came for
    • Be quick. Attention span is short. user shouldn't notice
    • make it easy for users to reach things while using their phones. Many people use a one handed grip this pretty much means don't put common buttons on the top.
    • If things load quickly, people are a lot more likely to stay
    • Biggest contributor to speed is networks

      • Speed

      • Reduce image usage
      • lower bandwidth
      • optimize and minify css and js files. Including eliminating whitespace.

    Primary controls

    • Put controls on the bottom of the screen where it is easy for thumbs to reach
    • Example of this is instagram (not facebook)
    • Having a "menu" wrapped in border and not a hamburger button is much easier for users
    • Users won't often complain, they will just leave the sit

    Mobile Inputs

    • Make forms easier. Ditch labels and put text inside the input field
    • make input box larger so it is easier to hit with a thumb.
    • make checkboxes larger as well
    • Eliminate the necessity of long forms
    • if a long form is necessary. Then divide the form up into different sections
    • If form is long, enable "autocomplete" on the form.
    • Another type of input is "passwords"
    • one way is to show the password by default, keep option to hide but most people aren't being snooped on
    • Space credit card information correctly.
    • give users the best keyboard for inputs. If you are asking for a phone number, only bring up the number pad instead of the regular keyboard
    • link phone numbers instead of making them copy and paste the numbers

    Mobile Advertising

    • Ad spending went from 25billion to 150billion in a 5 year period ending in 2017
    • Never do a pop up add. One of the most detested things on the web
    • Ads should be small and simple. Should not take up 40% of the mobile screen
    • A larger add could be revealed once clicked. But keep add small.
    • could have an add behind the page with a little window that shows the background when scrolled
    • Users don't visit your website to look at ads. Give them what they came for and then you can advertise to them
